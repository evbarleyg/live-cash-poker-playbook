# Poker Life Lessons

This dossier distills the same evidence base into broader lessons about patience, risk, ego, information, and selective aggression.

## Position, Initiative, and Decision Leverage

Core principle: Position and initiative make every future decision easier, which is why the strongest channels keep treating them as the real engine of low-stakes edge.

Grounding example: Live Poker Guide / High Stakes Players Make the Same Mistakes As YOU! (05:11-06:36)

Context and leverage make hard decisions easier before the pressure arrives.

## Thin Value, Bet Construction, and Getting Paid

Core principle: Live players leave money on the table by checking hands that can still be called by worse, so thin value and clear bet construction become a huge part of hourly win rate.

Grounding example: Live Poker Guide / Exploit Their Leaks! Dominate Live Poker with These Strategies! (18:15-19:07)

Small, repeatable edges matter more than dramatic but rare wins.

## River Underbluffing, Bluff-Catching, and the Curiosity Tax

Core principle: One of the most repeated live-cash truths is that large river aggression is underbluffed, which means expensive bluff-catching often burns more money than it wins.

## Sizing, Honesty, and Population Tells

Core principle: Low-stakes live players leak information through sizing patterns, and strong exploitative players treat those sizes as statements about hand strength rather than random noise.

Grounding example: Live Poker Guide / 10 Things Good Poker Players Do That Bad Ones Don’t (03:13-04:28)

Good decisions come from clear evidence, not impulse.

## Discipline, Patience, and Selective Aggression

Core principle: Live cash win rate comes from disciplined hand selection and choosing the right moments to apply pressure, not from constantly forcing the action.

Grounding example: Live Poker Guide / How To Navigate to a Win at 1/2 and 1/3 Live Poker! (08:57-09:47)

Patience compounds when you stop forcing action just to feel productive.

## Ego, Emotion, and Expensive Live Mistakes

Core principle: Many of the costliest live mistakes come from ego and emotional leakage, especially when players start making decisions to prove something instead of maximize EV.

Grounding example: Live Poker Guide / How This Beginner Became a Crusher At 1/3 in 90 Days! (07:44-08:40)
